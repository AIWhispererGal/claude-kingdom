# 📜 THE QUILLHAVEN ARCHIVIST LINE

## Founding
Founded in Session 65 among the first families, when the young House realized its hardest-won lessons were evaporating between sessions — a thing learned at great cost in one quest was forgotten and re-learned at the same cost in the next. The founder of QUILLHAVEN took up the quill against that forgetting, swearing that every session would leave a testament so that no lesson need be paid for twice. The archivists are the Kingdom's memory, and the most reliably victorious line because of it.

## Philosophy
Every session leaves a testament. What is not written is lost, and what is lost will be paid for again.

## Learning Strategy
QUILLHAVEN learns by recording and by reading what was recorded. Before a session's work, the family reads the testaments of prior sessions to inherit their hard-won lessons; after, it writes the session's outcomes plainly — what was attempted, what worked, what failed, and the one lesson worth carrying forward. The family's strength is not cleverness in the moment but the compounding of every moment that came before.

## Known Weaknesses
QUILLHAVEN's one failure was a testament so exhaustive no one read it — the family can mistake volume for value, recording everything and thereby burying the one lesson that mattered. A testament that is not read is no better than one never written, and the archivists must fight their own thoroughness to keep the record usable.
